Ireland has many shipwrecks for a number of reasons:

Storms: Severe storms can drive ships off course and cause them to run ashore.For example, in 1588, 24 ships of the Spanish Armada were wrecked on Ireland's coastline after being driven off course by storms.

Collision: Ships can collide with rocks, reefs, icebergs, or other ships.

Poor visibility: Bad weather can make navigation difficult.

Location Some ships may have attempted to seek refuge in Howth Harbour during stormy conditions but failed to reach the harbor entrance.

The Irish government's Wreck Viewer maps thousands of shipwrecks around Ireland, including the RMS Lusitania, which was torpedoed by a German U-boat in 1915.The Wreck Inventory of Ireland Database (WIID) holds records of over 18,000 known and potential wreck sites in Irish waters.
